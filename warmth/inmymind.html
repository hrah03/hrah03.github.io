Book: Lee O-Young's Last Interview
Author: Kim, Ji-Soo

Overview: In the fall of 2019, an article titled “The Last Interview with Lee O-Young” was published as part of Kim Jisoo's Interstellar series. In the article, the public embraced Lee’s message, where he declared, “My life has been a gift.” This interview gathered immense attention and ultimately became the decision for producing a book which delves even deeper into Lee’s final thoughts through a series of sixteen interviews conducted over the span of a year.
In Lee’s dialogues, he touches upon themes of love, forgiveness, religion, and science, all while delivering a resonant message: “Death lies at the heart of life.”

Book: Beloved
Author: Toni Morrison

Overview: Set in the period after the American Civil War, the novel centers upon the life of Sethe, an escaped enslave woman. The narrative weaves together themes of slavery, motherhood, trauma, and inescapable grip of memory.
Sethe is haunted by the ghost of her dead daughter, Beloved, whose death was the tragic consequence of Sethe’s desperate act to prevent her children from being enslaved. When a young woman named Beloved mysteriously appears, Sethe confronts her past, guilt, and the enduring scars of slavery.

Book: Kitchen
Author: Yoshimoto Banana

Overview: A beautifully meditative, tender book that explores themes of loss, grief, love, and the healing power of food and connection (emotional recovery).
The story follows Mikage Sakurai, a young woman who, after the death of her grandmother, finds solace in kitchens and cooking. As Mikage grapples with her grief, she forges deep emotional bonds with Yuichi. Together, they delicately navigate through their personal losses and healing, with kitchens and food becoming their symbolic refuges of warmth.
The novel reflects on the transient nature of life and the quiet beauty found in everyday moments.
